The Small Business 


		
		A small business is a privately owned, for-profit entity that meets revenue or employee standards,
		 as defined by the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA).
		 The SBAâ€™s standards vary by industry, but generally, a small business:
	
		Has fewer than 500 employees for manufacturing businesses Has fewer than 
		$7.5 million in annual receipts for most non-manufacturing businesses
		Is an independent business, not a subsidiary or affiliate of a larger company
		Industry-Specific Size Standards
		The SBA establishes size standards on an industry-by-industry basis,
		using the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) codes.
		 For example:														
		
		
		Agriculture: $0.75 million average annual revenue
		General and heavy construction: $28.5 million average annual revenue	
		Special trade contractors: $12 million average annual revenue			
		Wholesale trade: 100 employees											
		Retail and service: $6 million average annual revenue
		Key Characteristics
		Small businesses are typically characterized by:
		
		Limited scale and scope
		Family ownership or independent ownership
		Flexibility and adaptability
		Often innovative and entrepreneurial in nature
		May not dominate their field or industry
		Government Support
		Small businesses are eligible for various government programs and benefits, including:
		
		SBA loan programs
		Government contract opportunities
		Research grants
		Tax credits and incentives
